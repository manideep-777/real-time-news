Daily Governance Report 11-06-2025

1. Major issues highlighted in Print & Electronic Media:
Politics
Religious issues are increasingly influencing Andhra Pradesh's political landscape. Congress leaders in Nizamabad express dissatisfaction after a cabinet expansion, while YS Sharmila accuses Jagan Mohan Reddy of aligning with the BJP. The TDP advocates for incentives for larger families, and protests occur regarding women's safety and a journalist's arrest.
Infrastructure
Andhra Pradesh aims to complete 1,040 km of road works by 2025, a project valued at Rs 20,067 crore. The state also plans to build a Rs 4,000 crore Quantum Valley, modeled after Silicon Valley. Road work in Kakinada is halted due to contractor issues, causing distress among residents.
Education
A Telangana student topped the AP EAPCET 2025 exam. Secondary grade teacher transfers in Andhra Pradesh will now be handled through manual counseling instead of online. SC/ST categories achieved a full pass rate in both streams of an unspecified exam, with high pass rates for Christian and Muslim candidates.
Crime
Andhra Pradesh police arrested seven individuals in a minor girl gang rape case and a tiles worker for allegedly murdering a tribal girl. A woman was allegedly strangled by her husband in Saroornagar. The Chief Minister urged swift trials and harsh punishment for those involved in recent crimes against women.
Economy
Andhra Pradesh received Rs 9.2 lakh crore in investments over 11 months. EPACK Prefab opened a new sandwich panel manufacturing facility near Tirupati with a Rs 56 crore investment. The Telangana-Karnataka zone has the highest number of fraudulent tax deductions after Mumbai.
Environment
The TTD approved a Rs 4 crore greening project for Tirumala. Desperate fishermen torched invasive 'Rakashi' fish in Cherlapally lake to protect their livelihood and the ecosystem. Heavy rains are expected across Andhra Pradesh from June 11th, following thundershowers in north Andhra.
Social Issues
YSRCP women leaders protested in Nellore over rising atrocities against women. Landless Dalits in Salaarnagar face difficulties due to Dharani errors, preventing them from benefiting from Bhu Bharati schemes.
