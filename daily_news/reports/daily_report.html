Daily Governance Report 14-06-2025

1. Major issues highlighted in Print & Electronic Media:
Governance Failures
The Andhra Pradesh government faces criticism for delays in road works in Kakinada, leading to public suffering.

Landless Dalits in Salaarnagar are left out of Bhu Bharati land distribution due to Dharani system errors.

Congress leaders from Nizamabad express unhappiness after a cabinet expansion overlooks a four-time MLA.
Corruption
The Telangana-Karnataka zone has the highest number of fraudulent tax deductions after Mumbai, indicating potential widespread tax evasion in the region bordering Andhra Pradesh.
Political Unrest
Religious issues are increasingly dominating political narratives in Andhra Pradesh, potentially fueling social divisions.

YSRCP protested against the TDP government over the rise in atrocities against women, highlighting governance failures in protecting women's safety.

YS Sharmila accused YSRCP chief Jagan Mohan Reddy of aligning with the BJP, causing political friction.
Infrastructure
Andhra Pradesh aims to complete 1,040 km of road works in 2025, a project valued at Rs 20,067 crore.

The Andhra Pradesh Cricket Association plans to erect cricket stadiums in 25 districts within two years.

The state government plans to develop Amaravati's Quantum Valley, a Rs 4,000 crore project, to rival Silicon Valley.
Law & Order
Seven people were arrested in a minor girl gang rape case in Sri Sathya Sai district.

A tiles worker was arrested for allegedly murdering a tribal girl after she rejected his marriage proposal.

Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister urges for swift trials and harsh punishment for those involved in the recent murders and gang rape of young girls.
Education
Secondary grade teachers, language teachers, and physical education teachers demanded manual counseling for transfers instead of online, highlighting concerns with the current system.

SC/ST categories achieved a full pass rate in both agricultural and other streams in certain examinations, showcasing success in education but also revealing disparities.
Natural Disasters
Heavy rains are expected across Andhra Pradesh from June 11, according to the IMD, with thundershowers already lashing north Andhra.

The IMD also warns of rain, thunderstorms, and gusty winds over coastal and interior AP.
